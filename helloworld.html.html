Birmingham Rollers

Bird Facts Birmingham Roller Pigeon

The Birmingham Roller was bred in England with the ability to do rolling or somersaults in the air. These birds will fly high up in the air and when they are ready, they will do backwards somersaults.

There are several types of roller pigeon including:

  • The Birmingham
  • Galati
  • Oriental
  • Parlor roller pigeons

For more information on roller pigeon visit here: Roller Pigeon

  1. Birmingham roller pigeons were bred in Birmingham to somersault backwards whilst in flight.
  2. The Parlor roller pigeon cannot fly, but rolls on the ground instead.
  3. There are around 10,000 Birmingham roller pigeon breeders all over the world.
  4. Roller pigeon breeders can be found in a number of places, including urban, rural and desert areas.
  5. Roller pigeons are used in rolling competitions where the birds are judged on their rolling abilities either in the air or on the ground.
  6. The roller pigeon eats a bird feed made of mixed grain.
  7. Parlor roller pigeons are judged in competition by how far they can roll along the floor.
  8. The Oriental roller pigeon can stay in the air for hours and reach a height of 1,000 metres.
  9. There is a Birmingham Roller Pigeon World Cup competition that takes place every year.
